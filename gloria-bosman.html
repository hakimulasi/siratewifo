    Gloria Bosman (born 31st October 1973, age: 50 years when died) was a popular Jazz, African, Rock, and Gospel Vocalist, Producer, Composer, Vocal Coach, and Music Educator from South Africa. She has remained famous among her fans for winning the Best Newcomer award at the South African Music Awards when she launched her debut album ‘Tranquility’ in 1991. Just like this her songs ‘Too Much heaven’, ‘Play me The Love Songs’, ‘We Stand Together’ and others remained famous among her fans.

    She has been noted by many popular composers, musicians, directors, and producers for her appearance in the KKNK, Aardklop, Skousple, and much another music festival of all time in South Africa.

    In her long successful career as a vocalist, she has performed in countries like Angola, Nigeria, Namibia, Zimbabwe, Lesotho, Swaziland, London, France, Finland, Amsterdam, Australia, Chile, Argentina, Peru, and Venezuela. But this legendary singer of South Africa died on 14th march 2023.

    Gloria Bosman died due to a long Illness

    On 14th March 2023, the whole music industry of South Africa mourned the death of singer Gloria Bosman. She died at the age of 50 only. Her death news was confirmed by her family member when they made an official statement.

    “After a short illness, she transcended peacefully at her home, surrounded by family,” the family said in a statement. “Gloria had devoted her life, not just to her family, but to her music, she was loved and adored by many here in South Africa and beyond its borders.”

    Later after her death confirmation Family shared about her funeral services which were organized at Soweto Theatre, Cnr Bolani Link and Bolani Road, Jabulani, Soweto, 1868 on 17th March 2023

    Sharing about Gloria Bosman’s birthplace then she was born in Mofolo, Johannesburg. Later she was raised by her family in Pimville, Soweto. her birthdate was 31st October 1973.

    Gloria Bosman begins singing in the Church Choir. Along with learning singing skills, she gained a good education from African Institute.

    On her LinkedIn, she disclosed that she went to TUT Ishwane to get training in Opera singing and vocal art. She ended her high school at Lofentse Girls High School.

    Gloria grabbed a diploma from TUT (Tshwane University of Technology), Pretoria in Vocal Art and Opera.
