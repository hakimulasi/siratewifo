    Early Life

    Watt was born in 1989 on 22nd March to Connie and John Watt. His birthplace was Waukesha, Wisconsin. Their family is of Scottish ancestry. His mother’s and father’s careers are a building operations vice-president and a firefighter, respectively.

    He has two younger brothers, namely Derek Watt and T. J. Watt. The former is a fullback for the Los Angeles Chargers while the latter plays linebacker for the Pittsburgh Steelers. As for him, his initial interest was hockey though he also played football.

    Watt went to Pewaukee High School. He later earned a scholarship from Central Michigan University. Then, J. J. left the school to join the University of Wisconsin-Madison. He missed his senior year at the university. Instead, he chose his football career over completing his education.

    Career

    After accepting a scholarship from Central Michigan University. He was playing as a tight end. After 14 games, he had made eight receptions and 77 receiving yards. That is when his coach proposed that he move to offensive tackle.

    He did not welcome the suggestion well. Therefore, he left the university and joined the University of Wisconsin. There he would play as a defensive end. He played for the team for three years. It is during his senior year that he entered the NFL Draft. The Houston Texans selected him. In 2011 on 28th April, Watt signed a four-year contract with the team. Watt has been playing for the team ever since.

    Awards & Achievements

    It is evident that Watt is a great player. He has received awards such as Lott Trophy. In the same year mentioned above, he was both First-team All-American and First-team All-Big Ten. Also, he was the NFL sacks leader in 2012 and 2015. Watt was awarded the Bert Bell Award in 2014 as well.
