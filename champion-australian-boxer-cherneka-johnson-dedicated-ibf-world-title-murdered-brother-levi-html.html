    Champion Australian boxer Cherneka Johnson dedicated IBF world title to murdered brother Levi

    Cherneka Johnson had to overcome one of the worst cuts many boxing fans had seen to retain her world championship belt on Sunday - but her toughest blow came weeks ago when she lost her brother in tragic circumstances.

    24 Aug 2024, 00:00

    Cherneka Johnson had to overcome one of the worst cuts many boxing fans had seen to retain her world championship belt on Sunday - but her toughest blow came weeks ago when she lost her brother in tragic circumstances. 

    Johnson successfully defended her IBF world title in a brave, bloody contest against fellow Australian Susie Ramadan that many viewers thought should have been stopped on the undercard of the George Kambosos v Devin Haney rematch at Rod Laver Arena.

    Cherneka Johnson (pictured with her IBF world title belt) had to overcome one of the worst cuts many boxing fans had seen to retain her world championship belt on Sunday at Rod Laver Arena

    Despite having her vision obscured at times, Johnson kept her cool and outfought her opponent to win a unanimous points decision against opponent Susie Ramadan

    Incredibly, her opponent accused her of using performance enhancing drugs after the fight, saying the 27-year-old needs to be tested because she'd been told 'she's on it'.  


    Blood-soaked Johnson had other things on her mind, however, dedicating the amazing victory to her late brother.

    'I'm dedicating this to my brother and I'm so glad I got the job done - I know he's here in my corner,' announced Johnson after the fight.

    Johnson's 23-year-old brother Levi was fatally stabbed outside a Brisbane gym in September in a brutal bikie murder.

    Johnson dedicated the incredible win to her late brother who was murdered outside a Brisbane gym just a month ago

    Four assassins cornered the young man while he was leaving a car park and allegedly rammed his car. Levi tried to escape, but was allegedly chased down and knifed by the attackers.

    Johnson was photographed in her parent's driveway soon afterwards - often with her head in her hands.

    She wrote on her Instagram at the time: 'My Brother you brought so much love, happiness, laughter, challenge, adventure, tears and headaches to all those around you. 

    'That cheeky smile was contagious and I hope you continue to share it with those up in heaven.

    Johnson (pictured) could have called her off her upcoming title defence against Susie Ramadan and nobody would have thought less of her - but she decided to press onward

    'May you watch over us, show us your love and protection.

    'Fly high & party hard brother.

    'Forever in our hearts ♥️ I love you!'

    Johnson could have called her off her upcoming title defence against Susie Ramadan and nobody would have thought less of her - but she decided to press onward.

    'It was hard, it was a hard time during that week with my family,' Johnson said, fighting back tears.

    Johnson pictured with her brother and sister when they were children playing with a snake in a wildlife park

    'I always knew this is what my brother would have wanted. He wanted to be here on Sunday for this fight. This would have been the first fight he would have been able to come to, so this one is a dedication to him.

    'For me, I really just zoned in and used tunnel vision to just zone in on this fight.'

    Sunday's championship bout in Melbourne was Johnson's first defence of the title she had claimed in April against tough Mexican boxer Melissa Esquivel.

    After beating Esquivel, Johnson called out Aussie boxing golden girl Ebanie Bridges. 

    'Ebanie Bridges, if you'd like to come up a division (in weight) or I'm happy to come down,' she said.

    Johnson's only loss so far has been to Aussie Shannon O'Connell - who will challenge another Australian Ebanie Bridges for the IBF bantamweight title in December.

    'It's exciting to see women's boxing grow, and I'm excited to showcase my skills and defend my title,' Johnson said.
