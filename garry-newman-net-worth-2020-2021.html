    Career

    Garry Newman founded the Facepunch Studios in 2004, before the release of ‘FaceWound.’ In the same year, he began developing the game ‘Garry’s Mod.’ It was initially a modification for ‘Half Life-2’, a game created by the Valve but was later made into a standalone game.

The studio then decided to release ‘Garry’s Mod,’ which went on to become its primary flagship creation. By the end of 2016, the game had sold close to ten million copies worldwide and was one of the most played games on Steam, a digital video game service. Newman himself spent 338 hours in completing the game and made the achievement “Yes, I am the real Garry,” impossible to complete.

    The company then developed another action based multi-player game called ‘Rust.’ Although heavily criticized for the amount of savagery, the game became a more significant success than ‘Garry’s Mod.’

    In 2014, Newman announced that Facepunch Studios would be bringing out a prehistoric game titled ‘Before.’ However, in February 2019, the chief developer of ‘Before,’ Bill Lowe tweeted that the project had been terminated.
