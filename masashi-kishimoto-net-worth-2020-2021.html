    Early Life

    Career

    In 1995, Kishimoto submitted his first manga pilot to Shueisha. It was titled Karakuri. He worked together with an editor called Kasuke Yahagi. Most of their drafts ended up being rejected, including Michikusa as well as Asian Punk. His one-shot Naruto’s version, which he wrote in 1997, was published in Akamaru Jump Summer.

    In December of that same year got a shot with weekly shonen jump. He revised Karakuri, which was released for the first time in 1998. Unfortunately, it was not a huge success.

    After the setback, he shifted to seinen with two drafts, namely Baseball King and Mano, which concerned baseball and Mafia, respectively. His editor wanted him to give shonen genre a second chance. Therefore, he embarked on Magic Mushroom. Later, Yahagi suggested the serialization of storyboards. They began working on Naruto. It premiered in a weekly shonen jump in September 1999. Initially, it clinched position 43, but soon after that turned into a hit. Fifteen years later, with a collection of over 700 chapters. In 72 volumes, Naruto came to an end in 2014 on tenth November.

    Thanks to Naruto, Yuuchi Itakura, as well as Osamu Kajisa, who used to be his assistants, have gotten a share of their success. His other films are The Last: Naruto the Movie and Road to Ninja: Naruto the Movie in 2014 and 2012, respectively. In the former, Masashi was the character designer, chief story supervisor, and the owner of that original story. For the latter, he was the character designer, as well as the story planner.

    Back in 2010, Kishimoto produced a baseball manga titled Bench. Between April and July 2015, his series titled Naruto: Seventh Hokage and Scarlet springs. Besides manga, Kishimoto has written films, art books, novels, among others.

    Other manga includes Boruto: Naruto Next Generation, Mario, and Naruto: The Path Lit by the Full Moon. Also, Kishimoto has been part of Tekken 6, Naruto: Shippuden: Ultimate Ninja Storm Revolution, Motion Comic: Naruto and Educational Manga: The History of Japan Volume 1.
