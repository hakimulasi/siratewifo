    Early Beginnings and Education

    Born in the serene town of High Wycombe, Anna Lapwood emerged into a family deeply rooted in caring and cultural enrichment. Her early exposure to music, guided by her parents’ professions, set the stage for a flourishing career.

    Attending the Junior Royal Academy of Music was a pivotal chapter, where she not only honed her skills in multiple instruments but also embraced the complexities of the organ, an instrument that would later define her career.

    Director of Music at Pembroke College, Cambridge

    Appointed in 2016, Anna Lapwood became the Director of Music at Pembroke College, Cambridge, at just 21 years old. This role marked her as one of the youngest individuals to ever direct a choir at an Oxford or Cambridge University College.

    Founder of Pembroke College Girls’ Choir

    In 2018, Lapwood founded the Pembroke College Girls’ Choir, which was designed to provide choral education to girls from local schools aged 11–18.
