    Artie Lange Overview and Wiki

    Early Life and Career Beginnings

    Artie Lange‘s journey began in Livingston, New Jersey, where he was born to Judy and Arthur Lange Sr. His early life was not without challenges, including a stint at Seton Hall University, which he eventually dropped out of. Lange’s career in comedy started modestly in Manhattan’s stand-up scene.

    Breakthrough and Challenges

    After Mad TV, Lange’s career took a significant turn. In 1997, he co-starred in the comedy film Dirty Work alongside Norm Macdonald, which opened up more acting opportunities for him. This period also marked his involvement in The Norm Show, where he played a recurring role.

    Despite these successes, Lange’s personal demons continued to shadow his professional achievements.

    The Howard Stern Show Era

    In 2001, Lange’s career trajectory took another major leap when he joined The Howard Stern Show. His tenure on the show not only amplified his fame but also enriched his experience in the entertainment industry.

    During this period, Lange released two comedy albums and ventured into filmmaking with Artie Lange’s Beer League in 2006. He also authored the book Too Fat to Fish in 2008, which became a bestseller.

    Turbulent Times and Recovery

    Despite his professional success, Lange’s personal life was fraught with challenges. His struggle with substance abuse led to a suicide attempt in 2010, after which he underwent rehabilitation.

    Post-recovery, he hosted The Nick & Artie Show with Nick Di Paolo from 2011 to 2013 and The Artie Lange Show until 2014. During this time, he released his second book, Crash and Burn.

    Recent Endeavors

    From 2015 to 2017, Lange hosted The Artie Quitter Podcast and took on a recurring role in the HBO series Crashing. He also co-hosted The Artie and Anthony Show with Anthony Cumia until 2018.

    That year, he released his third book, Wanna Bet? and subsequently took a hiatus from his career following a series of arrests and drug rehabilitation.

    After a period of sobriety, Lange resumed stand-up and launched Artie Lange’s Halfway House podcast before entering another hiatus in February 2022.

    Personal life

    From 2002 to 2006, he was in a relationship with Dana Cironi. In 2009, Lange’s personal life took a significant turn when he met Adrienne Ockrymiek at a tanning salon. This encounter led to a deeper relationship, and the couple eventually got engaged. However, their relationship ended in 2014.
